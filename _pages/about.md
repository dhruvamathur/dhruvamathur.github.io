Hi! I am a PhD Educational Leadership, Policy and Human Development student with a concentration in Higher Education at the [North Carolina State University](https://ced.ncsu.edu/). My research sits at the intersection of higher education governance, organizational politics, and emerging technology - with a particular focus on how institutions adapt to AI, how the AI will impact the labor market and its consequences for higher education.

Before joining NC State, I worked for six-years at the [Indian School of Public Policy](https://www.ispp.org.in/), New Delhi - teaching courses on public policy, and devising institutional strategy across domains. I have also worked at the [Overseas Development Institute](https://odi.org/en/) and have done research projects for the the United Nations Office on Drugs and Crime, and the London School of Economics. As I experienced how institutions operated, I became fascinated by organizational theory and studying why institutions do what they do. This drove me towards empirical research to study various facets of an organization - people, culture, and structures.

At NC State, I am currently studying faculty responses and norms to AI across disciplines at US universities, and separately, the political dynamics of budget cuts at universities using archival data from NC State's own history of dealing with budget cuts during the Great Depression using a new foundational theory on fiscal pressures. I am also a [Mansour Doctoral Fellow](https://grad.ncsu.edu/mansour-graduate-fellows/).
